Ambi Insect Encoder

s3g Ambi Insect Encoder 64 is a zero-input procedural instrument for spatial insect populations. As many as 64 independently timed voices use stridulation, tymbal-like snap trains, wingbeat harmonics, and resonant body models, then encode their moving positions directly to first-through-seventh-order ACN/SN3D.

The engine is a musical bioacoustic model, not a species-identification tool and not a sample player. Its controls preserve the separable time scales and sound-producing mechanisms found across insect communication while leaving room for imagined populations.

Workflow

  1. Insert the plugin on a 64-channel track and choose a factory preset.
  2. Select REGIME, then balance population ACTIVITY, TEMPERATURE, VARIATION, and COUPLING.
  3. Shape the nested phrase, chirp, and pulse clocks before tuning body pitch and texture.
  4. Place and animate the population with swarm motion, field origin, and environment controls.
  5. Follow the instrument with an ambisonic decoder or meter.

Insect Source

REGIME selects CHIRPERS, TRILLERS, CICADAS, FLYERS, TICKERS, or MIXED SWARM. TEMPERATURE scales biological clock speed. VARIATION separates individuals, while COUPLING pulls phrase timing toward a shared chorus without making every voice identical.

Call Structure

PHRASE RATE, CHIRP RATE, and PULSE RATE are nested clocks expressed in hertz. They separate long calling/rest cycles, repeated calls, and the syllable, scraper-contact, or rib-snap texture inside a call. BODY PITCH sets the much faster tuned wing carrier for stridulators and the wingbeat fundamental for flyers. CALL LENGTH and REST shape the resulting smooth gates.

Body and Tone

BODY PITCH and BODY SIZE tune the resonant wing, tymbal/abdomen, or flight body. WING mixes a dedicated radiating-wing layer: tuned scraper/file resonance for chirpers and trillers, coupled tymbal radiation for cicadas, and a phase-locked bilateral wing model for flyers. Flyer sound combines an integer harmonic stack, bounded cycle-to-cycle thrust and timing variation, thorax response, and body-size-dependent wake turbulence; small bodies remain relatively tonal while larger bodies develop a denser buzz. RASP, BRIGHTNESS, RESONANCE, and AIR balance scrape noise, spectral emphasis, resonant decay, and high-frequency radiation. Regime changes and presets use a short transition crossfade.

Model Basis

The synthesis separates excitation from radiation: scraper impacts, rib snaps, flight strokes, and contact taps drive wing, body, abdomen, air, and substrate resonators. Stable per-voice identity controls placement and individual variation; a separate audio-rate random stream supplies texture, so noise no longer jitters pitch, activity, or motion. Slow phrase decisions and pitch drift keep the population irregular without making individual wing cycles unstable.

Development uses short-window spectral and band-energy checks for wingbeat texture alongside longer temporal checks for phrase behavior. Flyer presets must retain more temporal detail than a pure tone at the same effective wingbeat frequency without becoming unbounded noise. These are deterministic validation measures, not a machine-learning stage. See Insect Bioacoustics on the central References page for the physiological, field-recording, and signal-analysis sources.

Swarm Motion

FIELD RATE, ROAM, COHESION, SCATTER, ORBIT, LIFT, and NEAR PASS move the population. Ground calls remain low, cicadas favor canopy strata, and flyers can orbit and approach the listener. INERTIA controls positional following. Point size and halo intensity show each voice's current call envelope.

Environment Field

PLACE selects MEADOW, FOREST FLOOR, CANOPY, MARSH, PORCH, GREENHOUSE, or INTERIOR WALL. ENV RETURN, ENV SIZE, ENV DECAY, and ENV DAMPING mix and shape the encoded environment response.

Presets

The 16 factory presets cover cricket choirs, katydid waves, cicada masses, mosquitoes, bees, beetles, interior ticks, layered forests, and mixed summer populations. Factory presets default to 3OA and -6 dB. RANDOM stays inside bounded, regime-aware ranges. SAVE and LOAD write portable .s3ginsect presets in ~/Music/s3g/Presets/Ambi Insect Encoder/.

Order and Routing

The output bus remains 64 channels wide. ORDER activates 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, or 64 ACN channels for 1OA through 7OA and clears the remainder.
