Ambi Vox Encoder

s3g Ambi Vox Encoder 64 is a 64-voice vocal vector-wavetable instrument with first-through-seventh-order ACN/SN3D output. It combines the VOT Encoder's interpolated 4 x 4 waveform field with vocal articulation, phrase-driven vector movement, MIDI, and spatial voice motion.

Workflow

  1. Insert the instrument on a 64-channel REAPER track.
  2. Choose FREE, MIDI, or BOTH.
  3. Select an internal wave set or load a mono WAV atlas.
  4. Set the ambisonic order, voice count, tuning, and envelope.
  5. Shape the vocal layer and enter a phrase when articulated vector movement is wanted.
  6. Follow the instrument with an ambisonic decoder or meter.

Vector Source

Each source bank is divided into sixteen tables arranged as a 4 x 4 field. Voice U/V positions blend nearby tables continuously rather than switching between them. SINE, CLASSIC, DIGITAL, and FORMANT provide internal starting banks.

LOAD accepts a mono WAV file and divides it into sixteen equal waveform regions. The pre-release package's VOX Wavetables folder provides Black Metal, Throat, Choir, and Animal source fields. A loaded user bank is embedded in the REAPER project state.

FIELD shows AED motion, VECTOR shows table interpolation and the selected voice waveform, and SCORE shows the editable U/V path.

Vocal Layer

  • RASP, BREATH, THROAT, and DRIVE set the main vocal body and edge.
  • JITTER, VOWELS, and SCOOP vary pitch and movement through the waveform field.
  • SHAPE changes the attack contour; ARTIC sets phrase and consonant influence.
  • CONS, PLOS, and SIBIL control consonant bursts, plosives, and high-frequency articulation.
  • PHRASE sets phrase-advance rate in free-running modes.

Phrase Articulator

The phrase field is a synthesis score rather than recorded speech. Vowels move the U/V target, consonants create short articulated events, repeated vowel pairs create longer holds, and spaces or punctuation create rests. In FREE and BOTH, the phrase advances from the internal phrase clock. MIDI note events also advance and articulate the phrase.

Motion and Neighbor Gates

MANUAL, ORBIT, FLOW, PATH, and PULSE move voices in the shared U/V and AED field. Motion can run freely or follow the REAPER transport. LINK couples timbral and spatial movement.

NEAR defines neighbor proximity and NBR sets how many nearby voices are required to open a free-running voice. The field draws those relationships so changes in the constellation remain visible.

Order and Routing

The output bus remains 64 channels wide. ORDER activates the corresponding ACN/SN3D channels from 1OA through 7OA and clears all higher outputs.

Follow the instrument with an Ambi Speaker Decoder, Ambi Stereo Decoder, Ambi Head Decoder, or Ambi Energy Visualizer.
