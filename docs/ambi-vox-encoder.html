Ambi Vox Encoder

s3g Ambi Vox Encoder 64 is a 64-voice voicebank and WORLD-based vocal instrument with first-through-seventh-order ACN/SN3D output. It maps a typed phrase across independently pitched and positioned voices.

Workflow

  1. Insert the instrument on a 64-channel REAPER track.
  2. Choose FREE, MIDI, or BOTH.
  3. Load a UTAU-style voicebank folder or a vocal WAV file.
  4. Enter a phrase, then set the voice count, scale, pitch spread, and timing spread.
  5. Follow the instrument with an ambisonic decoder or meter.

Phrase Source

The LOAD button accepts either a voicebank folder containing WAV files and oto.ini, or a single vocal WAV file. Every loaded voicebank alias is analyzed and resynthesized by WORLD before playback. The first load writes a persistent cache in ~/Library/Caches/org.s3g.s3g-dsp/AmbiVoxWorld; later loads reuse it until the source WAV changes. The included synthetic test bank is in examples/voicebanks/s3g-demo-synthetic.

Voicebank preparation caches five formant-preserving WORLD pitch anchors from two octaves below through two octaves above each alias's detected base pitch. Runtime voices select the nearest anchor, which keeps wide MIDI and scale intervals closer to the original vocal character.

Words are mapped to available voicebank aliases and romaji sample keys by a small English grapheme-to-phoneme layer. The phrase panel shows the resolved alias sequence and a live voice-1 timeline. SPEAK prioritizes phrase timing. SING holds syllables longer and keeps every voice on the same phrase sequence. TEXTURE treats loaded samples as a continuously moving source field.

TIME STEP delays each successive rendered voice by as much as 1000 ms; TIME DEV adds a repeatable deviation around that cascade. These controls retarget crossfaded delay taps. Moving them does not recompile or restart the phrase.

Voicebank Timing and Pronunciation

The renderer honors each oto.ini entry's offset, consonant/fixed region, cutoff, preutterance, and overlap. Consonant regions run at their original timing. Vowels can sustain after the fixed region, and adjacent aliases crossfade using their preutterance and overlap values. BLEND scales that transition. If a legacy CV bank supplies zeros for every timing field, Ambi Vox derives a short onset and a vowel-only sustain region instead of looping the complete recording.

A bank can override the built-in word mapping with an optional s3g-pronunciations.txt file in its root folder. Put one lower-case word and its alias sequence on each line:

fire=fa i ya
voices=bo ka ru
sorrow=so ro

Aliases may be separated by spaces, commas, semicolons, or vertical bars. Lines beginning with # are ignored. Every alias on a line must exist in the loaded bank for that override to be used.

Vocal Layer

  • ROOT and SCALE define the pitch grid.
  • SPREAD expands or contracts scale-degree relationships while keeping voices on the selected scale.
  • DEV adds a deterministic pitch deviation per voice; TUNE and TRANSPOSE shift the resulting field.
  • PORT sets pitch glide time. VIBRATO and VIB RATE add per-voice phase-offset vibrato, with full depth in SING.
  • FORM, EDGE, AIR, and DRIVE shape the reconstructed voice.

MIDI and Phrase Voices

In FREE, active points take scale degrees around the root. In MIDI, each held note is allocated to one voice; chords therefore become spatially distinct voices. Notes are quantized to the selected scale before pitch spread and deviation are applied. BOTH retains the free-running field and lets held MIDI notes take over allocated voices.

PVOC Timing

The phrase-panel RATE control sets spoken or sung syllable pacing. Word spaces and punctuation produce explicit short, medium, and sentence-length gaps. The WORLD-panel RATE, loop, freeze, and scrub controls shape continuous TEXTURE playback and do not warp aliases in SPEAK or SING.

STRETCH changes target syllable duration from 0.25x to 4x without changing the selected musical pitch; fixed consonant timing is preserved and vowels provide the flexible sustain region. TRANSIENT controls phase resets at consonant and attack boundaries; higher values preserve sharper attacks. For voicebanks, the phase vocoder handles residual tuning between cached WORLD pitch anchors rather than carrying the full pitch shift.

Motion

MANUAL, ORBIT, FLOW, PATH, and PULSE move voices in the AED field. Motion can run freely or follow the REAPER transport.

Order and Routing

The output bus remains 64 channels wide. ORDER activates the corresponding ACN/SN3D channels from 1OA through 7OA and clears all higher outputs.

Follow the instrument with an Ambi Speaker Decoder, Ambi Stereo Decoder, Ambi Head Decoder, or Ambi Energy Visualizer.
