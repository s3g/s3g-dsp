Spectral / Buffer Effects

These effects reorganize audio inside an FFT frame or a captured time buffer. They focus on material transformation rather than topology, panning, or encoded-field processing.

Spectral Spray

Spectral Spray 2ch/8ch scatters FFT bins while retaining a dry/wet path aligned to the transform latency. Its controls cover displacement, smear, hold, freeze, spectral holes, phase blur, feedback, and a frequency window. Use the 2-channel build for stereo material and the 8-channel build when every lane should receive the same spectral model.

Buffer Processor

Buffer Processor 8ch captures a moving audio window and subjects it to skips, reversal, crushing, error paths, and corrupted-window behavior. Lane relationship controls let the eight channels remain coordinated while addressing different parts of the buffer behavior.

Choosing a Family

  • Choose Spectral Spray for frequency-domain displacement, memory, or freeze.
  • Choose Buffer Processor for time-address discontinuities, reverse motion, repeats, and damaged playback windows.
  • Choose Spectral Topology Processor when an editable point network should drive the FFT transformation.
