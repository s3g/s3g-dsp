Ambi Environment Generator

s3g Ambi Environment Generator 64 synthesizes evolving soundscapes directly as first-through-seventh-order ACN/SN3D. It has no audio input and keeps a fixed 64-channel output bus while the selected order controls the active channels.

The instrument builds environments from interacting processes rather than replaying field recordings. Broad beds, discrete events, resonant particles, periodic calls, and mechanical components occupy independently moving field cells so the result remains spatially active without requiring source automation.

Scenes

SCENE selects a starting ecology: WOODLAND, WETLAND, SHORE, RAIN, URBAN, INDUSTRIAL, or INTERIOR. A scene changes the internal balance among layers; the layer controls remain available for reshaping or hybridizing it.

World Controls

  • ACTIVITY changes aggregate event density and motion.
  • EVOLVE changes the speed and depth of slow environmental variation.
  • NEAR FAR moves the perceived field from close and present toward distant, quieter, air-damped events.
  • SPACE broadens the diffuse bed and raises the ambisonic order used by diffuse material.
  • WIDTH controls directional order energy. At zero only the omnidirectional component remains; at full width all components through the selected order are retained.
  • SEED selects a reproducible realization of the environment.
  • ORDER selects 1OA through 7OA; channels above the selected order are cleared.

Listener and World

Each seed creates a different world-space geography. Environmental roles are assigned to different emitter regions, and the emitters can move independently. The listener follows a smooth closed walking path through that geography, producing direction, distance, level, and parallax changes before ambisonic encoding.

  • YAW, PITCH, and ROLL rotate the listener's head relative to the generated world.
  • WALK sets traversal speed.
  • DEPTH sets the size of the listener's walking path. Zero keeps the listener stationary.
  • MOTION controls independent emitter drift.

Layers

  • WIND combines propagated gust control, turbulent bands, and thresholded whistles.
  • RAIN combines a broad hiss with nonuniform, surface-colored impacts.
  • WATER combines moving wash with short rising resonant cavities.
  • FIRE separates lapping, hissing, and discrete crackle events.
  • INSECTS uses nested pulse and phrase timing rather than constant modulation.
  • MACHINE combines smoothed harmonic motion with a noisy mechanical component.

Field View

The center L square is the listener. The eight numbered squares show listener-relative procedural emitters. Color follows azimuth, elevation, and distance; outline strength follows local activity. Use TOP, SIDE, 3/4, zoom, or blank-field dragging to inspect the distribution. The camera setting is saved with the project.

Routing

Place the instrument on a 64-channel REAPER track. Decode it with Ambi Speaker Decoder, Ambi Stereo Decoder, or Ambi Head Decoder. Start with a conservative monitoring level: procedural transients can change as layer combinations and scene activity increase.
