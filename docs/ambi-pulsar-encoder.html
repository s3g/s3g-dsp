Ambi Pulsar Encoder

s3g Ambi Pulsar Encoder 64 is a zero-input pulsar and neural-synthesis instrument with direct first-through-seventh-order ACN/SN3D output. One master emission clock feeds three formant lanes. Every lane has an independent pulsaret waveform, formant, overlap, amplitude, trigger displacement, and valid Ambisonic position. A separate sixteen-node recurrent circuit can sound directly, close an audio-feedback loop around the lanes, or be captured into pulsaret, envelope, and FM tables.

Slow emission rates produce individual events and rhythms. Raising the same clock carries the result through beating textures into continuous pitch. The formant controls remain independent of that transition.

Workflow

  1. Insert s3g Ambi Pulsar Encoder 64 on a 64-channel REAPER track.
  2. Choose a factory state, then set EMISSION for the overall rhythm-to-tone timescale.
  3. Set each lane's FORMANT, OVERLAP, LEVEL, OFFSET, and PULSARET.
  4. Combine probability, burst/rest, sieve, and lane-distribution masks.
  5. Raise DIRECT to hear the neural circuit, then shape its signed ring and matrix feedback. After the capture meter arms, press CAPTURE to snapshot new pulsaret, envelope, and FM tables.
  6. Set the field center, width, scatter, orbit, air, and Doppler behavior.
  7. Select the Ambisonic order and follow the instrument with an Ambisonic decoder or energy meter.

Synthesis Model

The master clock uses a double-precision phase accumulator, so long renders do not acquire integer-sample timing drift. Pulse crossings retain their fractional-sample age when a lane voice starts. Each lane owns a fixed pool of overlapping voices; the oldest tail is replaced only if all sixteen slots are occupied.

Formant Lanes

The three lanes share the emission event sequence but generate independent audio objects. FORMANT controls the oscillator inside each window. OVERLAP expresses window duration as a multiple of the current emission period, allowing isolated classical pulsars below one and dense concurrent pulsarets above one. OFFSET displaces a lane by a fraction of the emission period.

Pulsaret choices are Sine, Triangle, Saw, Square, Overtone, Fold, Impulse, and Noise. Saw and Square use polynomial bandlimited edge correction, Overtone removes partials above its safe band, and Impulse uses a finite bandlimited impulse train. High and Ultra render the nonlinear Fold path at two or four times the host rate and reconstruct it through cascaded low-pass stages.

Neural Synthesis

The neural section is a Tudor-inspired recurrent sound circuit, not a literal emulation of Intel's 80170NX. Tudor's instrument used a 64-neuron analog chip with manually configured weights. This implementation follows the newer sixteen-node reduction described by nuPG: four clusters of four leaky tanh neurons use nominal R-C times of 80, 32, 9.5, and 2 ms.

Each cluster is a four-stage signed ring. A sparse signed matrix also mixes every cluster into the others. Slower clusters gate the faster rings, and SLOW > FAST varies their effective R-C time. First-order allpass memories put phase-bearing paths inside the feedback web. The topology therefore has the three ingredients highlighted by the feedback-network demonstration: nonlinear nodes, independently invertible paths, and phase or slew processes inside the loop.

Neural Capture Utility

The utility samples the running network at 4096 Hz into a half-second rolling window. CAPTURE derives three different 2048-point tables: fast bipolar activity becomes the pulsaret, rectified slow activity becomes the envelope, and a zero-mean mid/fast mixture becomes the FM contour. An initial snapshot is taken automatically once the window is full.

PULSARET and ENVELOPE blend those captured tables with the selected analytic waveform and envelope. FM DEPTH reads the captured FM contour across each grain and expresses its depth in semitones. Pulsaret and FM captures receive eight progressively filtered mip levels; playback selects and interpolates levels from the instantaneous phase increment. New captures crossfade over 80 ms so active grains never see a one-sample table swap.

Composable Masks

The event masks are applied together. PROB supplies stochastic density. BURST ON and REST form a repeating gate. SIEVE MOD and RESIDUE retain one residue class of the integer event sequence; modulo one disables the sieve. DISTRIB moves from all lanes firing together toward rotating one event through A, B, and C.

Spatial Model

Every formant lane is rendered to mono, conditioned, and then multiplied by one complete spherical-harmonic basis vector. Width, orbit, and scatter move those three valid source vectors; they never scale individual HOA channels independently. With width and motion at zero, all lanes collapse exactly to the selected center direction.

AIR applies distance-dependent high-frequency loss before encoding. DOPPLER adds a smoothed variable delay for radial movement. Leave Doppler at zero when the pulsar clock itself should remain the only timing structure.

Order and Routing

The output bus is always 64 channels wide. The selected order activates (order + 1)² ACN channels and clears the remainder: 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, or 64 channels for first through seventh order.
