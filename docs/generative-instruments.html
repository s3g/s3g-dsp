Generative Instruments

Generative instruments synthesize their source material internally. Some run without MIDI; others combine free-running voices with MIDI notes. Spatial instruments publish ACN/SN3D directly and are also organized under Ambisonic Encoders.

Discrete Multichannel

  • Fault: turns generated data or any file's literal bytes into a free-running or MIDI-playable eight-channel field of stochastic geometry, damaged codec models, and resonant wavefolding.

Ambisonic Synthesis

  • Ambi VOT Encoder: vector-wavetable synthesis with editable timbre paths.
  • Ambi Wave Terrain Encoder: contour-based oscillator scans through a procedural terrain.
  • Ambi Wind Encoder: filtered-noise weather, gust, material, and microweather synthesis.
  • Ambi Water Encoder: procedural currents, droplets, splashes, and bubbles.
  • Ambi Pulsar Encoder: multipoint pulsaret synthesis and neural waveform capture.
  • Ambi Stochastic Encoder: dynamic-stochastic generators, time-fields, and topology projection.
  • Ambi Wrangler Encoder: autonomous Benjolin/rungler-style logic synthesis.

Routing

Fault uses eight ordinary output lanes. The Ambi instruments use a fixed 64-channel bus with the active order occupying the first (order + 1)^2 channels. Follow those instruments with an Ambisonic Decoder or utility.
