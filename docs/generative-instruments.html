Generative Instruments

Generative instruments synthesize their source material internally. Some run without MIDI; others combine free-running voices with MIDI notes. Spatial instruments publish ACN/SN3D directly and are also organized under Ambisonic Encoders.

Discrete Multichannel

  • Fault: reads generated data or file bytes as moving waveforms, joins them into stochastic curves, and reshapes them with damaged codec models and resonant wavefolding.

Ambisonic Synthesis

  • Ambi VOT Encoder: interpolates among sixteen waveforms while voices follow editable paths through the resulting timbre field.
  • Ambi Wave Terrain Encoder: turns closed contour scans across a procedural surface into oscillator waveforms and spatial positions.
  • Ambi Wind Encoder: shapes broadband noise into gusts, turbulent air, and resonant material responses.
  • Ambi Water Encoder: combines continuous flow noise with modeled droplets, splashes, and resonant bubbles.
  • Ambi Insect Encoder: builds stridulating, tymbal, wingbeat, and mixed populations with coupled call clocks and swarm motion.
  • Ambi Pulsar Encoder: repeats short windowed waveforms from rhythmic rates into pitch, with recurrent-network waveform capture.
  • Ambi Neural Ecology: grows recurrent node populations inside a field whose adaptive ears learn where to listen and vote on movement through a behavioral lattice score.
  • Ambi Stochastic Encoder: uses second-order random walks to evolve breakpoint amplitude and duration curves.
  • Ambi Wrangler Encoder: couples oscillator pairs through comparator-driven feedback shift registers for repeating but unstable patterns.

Routing

Fault uses eight ordinary output lanes. The Ambi instruments use a fixed 64-channel bus with the active order occupying the first (order + 1)^2 channels. Follow those instruments with an Ambisonic Decoder or utility.
