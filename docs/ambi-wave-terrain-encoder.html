Ambi Wave Terrain Encoder

s3g Ambi Wave Terrain Encoder 64 is a 64-voice CLAP instrument with direct first-through-seventh-order ACN/SN3D ambisonic output. It adapts the field composition of Ambi Stochastic Encoder to wave-terrain synthesis: each voice selects a region of one shared AED terrain, scans a closed contour there, and uses that contour as its oscillator waveform.

The scan coordinates have two linked readings. Terrain height along the contour becomes sound, while each scan sample's longitude, latitude, and terrain height become azimuth, elevation, and distance. A voice therefore sounds and occupies the same place in the terrain model.

Workflow

  1. Insert s3g Ambi Wave Terrain Encoder 64 on a 64-channel REAPER track.
  2. Use FREE mode for a sustained terrain oscillator bank, MIDI mode for note-triggered voices, or BOTH.
  3. Choose a terrain SKIN, its relief controls, and a READ interpretation.
  4. Shape each contour with TRACE, RADIUS, ASPECT, ROTATE, and WARP.
  5. Compose movement with the selection LAW, transition JOIN, and time-field controls.
  6. Set ORDER, VOICES, and the AED projection controls.
  7. Follow with an ambisonic decoder or energy meter.

Terrain Controls

ControlMeaning
SKINSelects Harmonic, FBM, Cellular, VOT, Ridges, Dunes, Craters, or Tectonic surface construction.
DEPTHMorphs the scan profile from its smooth geometric carrier toward the procedural terrain.
ROUGHChanges the frequency and weight of the four terrain layers.
FOLDFolds terrain height for sharper peaks and more complex waveforms.
RELIEFScales both visible radial displacement and the terrain contribution to the scan profile.
READDerives the waveform from terrain Height, Edge, Curvature, or a Blend. Height is the factory default.
MIXBalances height with the selected differential interpretation in Blend mode.

Field Composition

TRACE chooses an Orbit, Lissajous, Rosette, or Fold contour. RADIUS, ASPECT, ROTATE, and WARP shape that contour around each selected region. Five progressively smoothed table levels reduce aliasing as oscillator pitch rises.

LAW chooses Random, Series, Weight, Tendency, Markov, or Walk region selection. JOIN determines whether a new region follows a direct link, merges with a neighboring voice, or varies locally. Density, duration, rest, contrast, deviation, memory, neighbor transfer, and macro duration compose activity over time. XFADE controls the current-to-next wavetable interpolation.

Voice Model

FREE mode sustains the stochastic voice field from BASE. SPREAD distributes pitch around that note, while DETUNE adds deterministic voice offsets. MIDI mode allocates the same voice pool from incoming notes and uses the ADSR controls. BOTH layers both behaviors.

Spatial Model

Voice positions are encoded directly into HOA from the active scan phase. Scan longitude maps to azimuth, latitude maps to elevation, and terrain height displaces distance. AZIM, ELEV, and DIST set the formation center, SPACE scales its angular extent, and FOLLOW smooths phase-driven spatial motion.

Display

TERRAIN VOICE FIELD uses neutral relief lighting for the shared surface. Every white line is an active contour moving across the terrain, and its bright point is the current scan phase. Click anywhere along a contour to select its voice; selection does not depend on catching the audio-rate head. The selected voice also shows faint cyan and magenta references for its current and next regions.

TERRAIN TO WAVEFORM places the sampled scan profile above the interpreted oscillator waveform. The profile begins with the contour's geometric carrier and morphs toward terrain height by DEPTH × RELIEF. A circular Orbit over a smooth sphere therefore produces a low-harmonic sinusoidal profile rather than retaining hidden terrain detail. In Height mode both graphs correspond directly apart from DC removal and normalization. Edge and Curvature are calculated from this displayed one-dimensional profile; Blend combines them.
