Direct Panning

Direct panners calculate speaker gains from source positions and write those gains directly to discrete output channels. They share one layout editor and preset language, but they do not create an ACN/SN3D bus.

Panner Family

  • Layout Panner: the main source-to-speaker panner with built-in arrays, custom speaker layouts, and saved camera views.
  • DBAP Panner: distance-based amplitude panning across the active speaker layout.
  • LBAP Panner: layout-based panning for two- and three-dimensional speaker relationships.
  • VBAP Panner: vector-base amplitude panning over valid speaker pairs or triplets.

Choosing Direct or Ambisonic Routing

Choose direct panning when the target speaker layout is known and the plugin should publish the final speaker feeds. Choose an Ambisonic Encoder when the spatial field should remain independent of the listening layout until a later decode stage.

Downstream Calibration

After a direct panner, use Speaker Array Utilities for main-speaker high-pass filtering, per-output delay, trim, mute, polarity, and subwoofer sends.
