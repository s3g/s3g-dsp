Ambi Stochastic Encoder

s3g Ambi Stochastic Encoder 64 is a 64-voice dynamic-stochastic CLAP instrument with direct first-through-seventh-order ACN/SN3D output. It can run freely, respond to MIDI, or combine both behaviors.

Each voice owns an evolving polygonal pressure curve with independent breakpoint amplitudes, timing, walk velocity, and rendered waveform state. A persistent nearest-neighbor topology gives every voice a distinct synthesis-space identity while energy, collisions, and delayed events pass between voices. The default FREE PERIOD configuration favors unstable, abrasive timbres and long-running variation.

Workflow

  1. Insert s3g Ambi Stochastic Encoder 64 on a 64-channel REAPER track.
  2. Choose FREE, MIDI, or BOTH.
  3. Select an ambisonic ORDER, voice count, stochastic system, and pitch range.
  4. Choose a dynamics mode, shape its topology mapping, then choose a spatial motion mode.
  5. Follow the instrument with an ambisonic decoder or meter.

Pressure Curves

The PRESSURE CURVE display shows the waveform and breakpoints currently used by the selected voice. Selecting another point reveals that voice's separate curve. Curve changes are prepared at cycle boundaries and crossfaded over the following cycle.

ControlMeaning
PTSSets 4 to 32 pressure-curve breakpoints.
AMPSets the size of the reflected random walk applied to breakpoint amplitudes.
TIMESets the random walk applied to the duration between breakpoints.
INERTControls how strongly each walk retains its previous direction.
A-DISTSelects the amplitude-step distribution.
T-DISTSelects the timing-step distribution independently.

The distribution menus provide UNIFORM, GAUSS, CAUCHY, LOGISTIC, ARCSINE, EXPON, and BINARY. Pairing unlike distributions can make contour and rhythm evolve at very different rates.

Models

ModelBehavior
DIRECTApplies first-order reflected steps directly to breakpoint amplitude and timing.
DELTAApplies a second-order walk to the change between successive breakpoint states.
CURVEDUses the delta walk with cubic interpolation between pressure points.
FREE PERIODLets the accumulated timing walk and topology state bend oscillator period around its target pitch. This is the most unstable and overtly dynamic model.

Systems

SystemBehavior
INDEPENDENTVoices evolve without energy feedback from other voices.
NEIGHBOREach voice responds to the measured energy of its nearest spatial neighbor.
FIELDThe complete field responds to global sound and kinetic energy.
NETWORKCombines local, global, kinetic, and delayed topology feedback.

COUPLE sets neighbor transfer and spring strength, MEM sets network persistence and cascade delay, and REACT sets how strongly topology affects waveform pressure, period, pitch, and voice gain.

Topology Dynamics

The dynamics layer runs independently of the GUI. It evolves persistent points distributed through a bounded synthesis space, with per-point velocity, two stable nearest-neighbor relationships, collision state, edge tension, crowding, and delayed network pulses. Each point retains its own anchor, so the map does not collapse when a transient meter decays. Motion continues when the editor is closed.

ModeBehavior
OFFUses the spatial motion engine without topology physics.
GASUses free particles, elastic collisions, and boundary rebounds without spring bonds.
NETAdds a two-neighbor elastic network whose bonds can stretch, break, and reform.
CASCADEUses a lighter network and passes delayed event pulses between neighboring voices.

DRIVE sets the target kinetic activity. BOUNCE sets collision restitution, DRAG dissipates motion, and RADIUS sets the point collision distance. Internal limits keep the system bounded without forcing it toward a static equilibrium.

Voices and Pitch

BASE sets the free-running center note. RANGE distributes active voices around it, while DEV adds deterministic per-voice tuning deviation. ACT changes the density of internally generated voice gates. MIDI notes use the shared ADSR envelope and their incoming velocity.

Stochastic Field

Each synthesis voice is also an ambisonic point. FIELD holds the starting distribution, ORBIT creates continuous circulation, DRIFT combines slow non-repeating trajectories, and FEEDBACK lets measured voice energy alter AED position and distance.

RATE, AMOUNT, and SPREAD shape motion around the center AZIMUTH, ELEV, and DIST. SPAT sets how strongly topology motion changes the actual AED position. Set it to zero to keep the spatial image independent while the synthesis topology continues evolving.

Topology Map

SYN sets how strongly topology reshapes each voice's pressure curve and event behavior. X controls pressure-curve mutation, Y controls event density and duration, Z controls register and free-period behavior, and distance from the center controls intensity and deviation. Collision impulse, velocity, crowding, edge tension, and delayed pulses act as secondary modulation rather than replacing those persistent coordinates.

The main display provides three linked views:

  • FIELD shows the true AED positions, neighbor links, collisions, and broken bonds.
  • MAP shows the persistent synthesis coordinates: X is mutation, Y is event behavior, Z is period and register, and radius is intensity and deviation.
  • LOOPS shows the live causal cycle from local energy through motion, contact, neighbors, field state, and curve mutation.

The spatial and synthesis views share one dynamics engine, but remain visually separate. SPAT projects the persistent topology into AED space without turning a spatial direction into a fixed timbral label.

Technical References

The model and distribution choices were informed by the documented behavior of Csound dynamic-stochastic opcodes and the SuperCollider stochastic generator family. The plugin uses its own bounded, cycle-crossfaded implementation for multichannel realtime operation.
